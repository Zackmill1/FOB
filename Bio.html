Biography

Fall Out Boy was formed in Wilmette Illinois, a suburb of Chicago in 2001. The band consists of Patrick Stump, Pete Wentz, Andy Hurley, and Joe Trohman. Originally, Fall Out Boy started as a side project by Wentz and Trohman. Stump joined shortly after, and Hurley after proving to be the best fit on drums.The groups debut album came out in 2003 titled Take This to Your Grave The first major label breakthrough album was 2005’s From Under the Cork Tree. with two singles, “Sugar We’re Goin’ Down” and “Dance, Dance” that went on to go double platinum. The next album the band produced was in 2007 titled Infinity on High. After the band’s fourth studio album, Folie a Deux, the boys took a hiatus with each member taking on side projects and a much-needed break. The band’s hiatus went from 2009-2012. In 2013, Fall Out Boy came back together to record and release their first album post-hiatus titled Save Rock and Roll. The band then released their sixth studio album in 2014, American Beuaty/American Psycho. Fall Out Boy’s most recent album was released in 2017 titled Mania.
